What do you do at a Toastmasters Meeting?

Conny Söderholm 05.06.2018
(Updated on 03.05.2023 )

Are you afraid of speaking in front of an audience? Do you feel uncomfortable giving presentations or attending meetings? Do you want to face your fears and become more effective in your communication with other persons? Then you should join Toastmasters!

At Toastmasters you can develop your communication and leadership skills. You can practice your speeches in a protected environment, and get feedback as well. You will see that your confidence and stage presence grows as you continue to practice.

 Toastmasters has over 352,000 members divided among 16,400 clubs in 141 countries, so you can find a club almost anywhere. In Helsinki, I recommend visiting my local club, Staditalkers where you can speak English, or why not try out the Finnish speaking club, Stadin Sanasepät. It is free to join as a guest, and you will be able to take part in the Table Topics, which is a 1-2min speech about the theme of the evening, freely formulated by you.

Where is the leadership skills then? There are several roles required to make a meeting function, and by accepting these roles you will slowly start to learn how to act as a leader. There is the Ah-counter, who counts and checks if you use any 'Uhs', 'Ums' or 'Aahs'. The Timekeeper times each speaker and meeting officer. The Grammarian checks for excellent use of language, while the Word of the Day role introduces new and interesting words to the club. The Toastmaster of the Evening runs the show, and it is through this role you really learn how to act as a leader and host for an event.

Go and check Toastmasters out and send me a message when you have attended your first meeting and tell me how it was! I am all ears. Why not come and say hi at our next meeting? You are welcome!
