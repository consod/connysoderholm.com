Table Topics Evaluation at Toastmasters

Conny Söderholm 12.06.2018
(Updated on 03.05.2023 )

Table Topics Evaluation at Toastmasters - Yesterday at our Toastmasters meeting with Staditalkers I was given the magnificent opportunity to evaluate our Table Topics speakers. Table Topics is an improvised mini-speech that lasts for 1-2 minutes. The topics are related to the theme of the evening and the speakers do not know what to speak about until just a few moments before or after they enter the stage. Read on to learn what a Table Topics evaluator does at Staditalkers.

Evaluating other speakers is after speaking itself the bread and butter of Toastmasters. After you have delivered a speech, you are often not sure of how you did and how you acted on the stage. As I was delivering my first speech, the Icebreaker in the club I did not notice that I kept my hands in my pockets because of nervousness. It is the evaluator's task to mention what you can improve on. The task of the Table Topics evaluator is to evaluate each mini-speech. There is usually time for about 5-6 speakers and the evaluation should be given in 2-4 minutes. The evaluation should be kept to the point, and be constructive.

I try to give evaluation according to the sandwich method. That means that you first praise the speaker for something he or she performed well, you are laying the first loaf of bread out. You proceed with mentioning one thing that they could think about improving in their next speech, adding the cheese. You then wrap up your evaluation by praising again, putting the top bread slice over the sandwich.

Me doing Table Topics Evaluation in the Sinebrychoff park.

One example evaluation could be that you praise the speaker for opening the speech with a question, thus engaging the audience. You could then proceed to suggest that you always should keep eye contact with the audience. The final praise could mention that the speech had a clear introduction, body, and conclusion. Well done!

I want to thank all our guests and members that bravely volunteered for speaking. Remember that you can enter the Table Topics as a guest, completely free! We also had a visiting member from the Trailblazers Toastmasters in Orlando. I want to thank him for visiting our club, it is always nice to chat with new people. I also learned from our visiting member that they do not have Table Topics evaluation in their club, and it was something that he was interested in bringing with him. Do you have Table Topics evaluations in your clubs? Personally, I think it is something that always should be on the agenda. See you at the next meeting!
